The Market Opportunity

America is short 3.8–7.2M homes.
The bottleneck is lots, not demand.

National builders have capital, buyers, and land pipelines. What they lack is entitled lots — the shovel-ready, subdivision-approved parcels they can start building on immediately.

Entitlement is slow (18-30 months), political, and capital-intensive. Banks won't fund it. Builders don't want the risk. This creates a gap that has no institutional solution.

64% of U.S. builders report "low or very low" lot supply — their single biggest constraint. (NAHB, 2025)

THE KEY ASYMMETRY

Capital Recycling
HOW RECYCLING WORKS:
When a deal exits (18-24 months), the fund's original capital (option premium + soft costs) is returned to LPs or redeployed into the next deal. This effectively multiplies the fund's investment capacity without additional LP capital — turning $10M into $14-18M of total deployment over the fund life.

Risk Management

Every risk has a structural
mitigation built into the model.

Risk
Structural Mitigation
🏗️
Entitlement failure
Option structure caps loss at 6–8% of land value. Deal dies early and cheap — never late and expensive.
🏛️
Municipal / political risk
Builder pre-validation and muni pre-consultation before any capital deployed. Kill SOP applied at earliest signal.
🏠
Builder takeout risk
LOIs secured pre-entitlement. Competitive tension from 2+ builders per deal — buyer is never speculative.
📈
Macro / rate risk
18–24 month cycles limit rate exposure. Housing supply deficit is structural (3.8–7.2M units) — not a cycle.
🗺️
Geographic concentration
Builder-guided market selection diversifies across multiple states, counties, and municipalities.
⚡
Kill rate overrun
90% kill rate is modeled conservatively into base case. Dead deals cost ~$50–90K — not the fund.
💧
Liquidity risk
Closed-end structure; LPs underwrite illiquidity. Capital recycling creates interim return events every 18–24 months.
